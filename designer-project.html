Designer at the End of…

Creative Coding • Web Design • Visual Identity • 2025

Context & Goal

What started as an individual school project exploring a rarely answered question of "What is design?" Later expanded into a group effort to create a website hosting the final works of the entire Communication Design VI class (2025), Bachelor's Degree in Communication Design, of FBAUL.

Challenge

How do we tell our story?

We set out to tell our story, as a group of three, exploring the concept of ubiquitous computing: the shift from digital as a separate space (internet, apps, devices) to something embedded in everyday life.

At the same time, we needed to understand how the other students very different student projects could fit within this narrative, while still having space to stand on their own, without being overpowered by our groups concept or aesthetic.

Research

As an experimental and conceptual project, grounding the work was essential. Starting by understanding that Ubiquitous Computing (Ubicomp), a term coined by Mark Weiser, describes a world where computers are no longer isolated objects but embedded in the environment. Walls become interfaces, books become data carriers, and cameras act as living archives.

We also looked at emerging interaction models, such as Google ATAP's Soli radar, which explores nonverbal, gesture-based interaction and Simon Weckert's 2020 intervention—pulling 99 smartphones in a cart—tricking Google Maps into creating a false traffic jam. Although the streets were empty, the system redirected drivers away, revealing how easily digital systems can reshape our behaviours and physical reality in society.

Process

Ideation

After the research phase and a review of the projects being developed across the class, a pattern emerged: student work begins to reflect personal beliefs, interests, and motivations more than expected. Over time, experience, environment, and shared references—books, texts, and theories—shape a collective identity, both as designers and as individuals.

This dynamic echoes what Vilém Flusser describes: "People will be in contact with one another, through their fingertips… forming a global network through the practice of design."

The class projects reflected both individual and shared concerns. Each group responded to the question "what is design, after all?" by filling in "A designer at the end of…" with different words, revealing a diverse set of perspectives that questioned specialization, fragmented categories, and growing uncertainty about the role of design.

Instead of resolving this fragmentation of ideas, we embraced it.

The website became a "Super Brain": a collective system where projects converge and remain in dialogue. Seemingly unrelated works connect, generating new relationships and meanings. The result is not a fixed narrative, but a network, where the whole emerges from the interaction between parts, rather than from any single project alone.

Deliverables

Digital poster and project teaser, The deliverables for this project include a digital poster and a project teaser as well as the final website, showcasing our original idea of ubiquitous design and the joining of all the classes' projects (the content of the website).

Final Website

The result is a techno-futuristic, open-source vision where knowledge is shared, preserved, and continuously reshaped, positioning design as a collaborative practice for the future.
